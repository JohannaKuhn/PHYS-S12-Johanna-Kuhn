<br> ## 2: Press Fit & 3D Modeling ### Press Fit: For this press-fit assignment, I designed pieces that would come together and make a tree. First I drew out my design and started planning the lengths and diameters of the shapes. <center><img src="week 2 tree design.jpg" width="520" height="750"></center> Then I opened Fusion360 and made a new project document. After that, I started inputting line lengths and circle diameters from my hand-drawn design. When I was finished I right-clicked on my sketch and exported it as DXF to my USB drive. After that, I laser cut it out and put the finished pieces together. <img src="tree_no_leaves.jpg" width="205" height="320"> <img src="leaves.jpg" width="205" height="320"> ______________________________________________________________________________________ ### 3D Modeling: For the 3D modeling part of the assignment, I started to model a perfume bottle. I started by measuring the lengths of all sides and for the rounded parts I measured the diameters. <center><img src="week 2 3D design.jpg" width="505" height="720"></center> Once I inputted all of these things into Fusion360 I had a couple of seperate cylinder shapes. With the move/copy tool I stacked them all on top of each other and added a hole on the toppest one. Now that the 3D shape was completed I modified the appearance of the bottle into glass and kept the top of the bottle metal. <center><img src="week 2 perfume.PNG" width="920" height="550"></center>> Next I made a wooden fork. First I inputed the general shape of it using the lengths of the sides and diameters of the round parts. After that I went back over it and added the measurements of the prongs on the forks. I trimmed away the lines that I didn't need and ended up with a good fork shape. After that I extruded the shape by 2 mm. Now that it was a 3D fork I changed it's appearance and made it a cherry wood. <center><img src="week 2 wooden fork.PNG" width="920" height="550"></center>
