About Me & My Final Project

<br> ## 3: Kinetic Scultpure Our assignment was to build a kinetic sculpture using circuitry. I came up with a boat that would rock back and forth using wire and a motor, so I drew out my design first. <img src="boat design.jpg" width="330" height="420"> After designing it I redrew it in Fusion360. ## For the boat shape: I used a diameter of 152.40 mm and then split it in half to make two semi-circles. I created a hole with a diameter of 1.00 mm centered 38.1 mm from the bottom of the shape and 38.1 mm from the side of the shape. For the underside of the boat shape I halfed the circumferense of the circles to make the length of the rectangle shape and the width would be 76.20 mm. For the top of the boat shape I made another rectangle with the measurements of 76.20 mm by 152.40 mm with a hole with a diameter of 1.00 mm centered in the center of the shape. ## For the sail shape: I made a triangle with I hieght of 76.2 mm and a width of 152.4 mm. I placed a line down the midpoint to create two symetrical triangle. I plan on putting a wire in between the two triangles to make the finished sail. ## For the box shape: For the base i used the measurement of 279.4 mm by 203.2 mm. I made the sides with 2 rectangles measured 203.20 mm x 101.60 mm and 2 rectangles measuring 279.40 mm x 101.60 mm. <img src="boat fusion.PNG" width="920" height="550"> ## Making a built in place for the motor and curcuit: For the supports I used the diameter of the round end of the motor in Fusion360 and then offset it outwards by 5.00 mm. I found the midpoint of the outer most line and place a rectangle with the measurement of ___ into it. For the other side I used the length and width of the rectangular end of the motor (18.76 mm x 22.35 mm) and offsetted that number by 5.00 mm. I added the same rectangle to the midpoint of the __ mm side and trimmed the unneeded lines. <img src="motor holder.PNG" width="920" height="550"> ## Added legs to the bottom of the box: I wanted a place to put the wires and boards neatly so I made a hollow leg where the wires could sit in underneath the sculpture. I used simple rectangles to make them. Using multiple rectangles that measured 45 mm x 79 mm and 45 mm x 37mm. <img src="boat box legs.PNG" width="920" height="550"> # Finished Product <video src="finished boat.mp4" class="object-fit-fill" autoplay width="820" height="450"></video>
